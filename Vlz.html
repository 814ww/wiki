Lily Angelina Velez

Lily Angelina Velez (January 12, 2008 – August 15, 2091) was a distinguished Filipino-American artist, photographer, and government official who left an indelible impact on the fields of art and international public service. Known for her intellectual versatility and charismatic presence, Velez held the esteemed role of General Advisor for Northern Ireland, where she worked closely with her husband, Lukas Arthur Alfaro, a respected general attorney. Born in Los Angeles, California, to a Filipino father and an American mother from Michigan, Velez’s life was marked by cultural diversity, a deep passion for art, and a commitment to social and civic causes. Growing up with three sisters, Velez spent her formative years in Los Angeles before moving frequently, residing in locales such as Washington, D.C., and Manila, Philippines, a city she cherished and often referred to as her "home at heart."

Early Life

Raised in a vibrant multicultural household, Velez demonstrated a proclivity for creativity from an early age, immersing herself in arts, crafts, and track racing. She attended the International School Manila (ISM) during her teenage years, where she honed her artistic talents, creating posters for the school and dedicating countless hours to sketching and sculpting. Known among her friends for her indie aesthetic, she crafted DIY accessories and experimented with makeup, drawing inspiration from artists such as Beabadoobee. Her friendships with Brielle Bitanga, Annika Tang, and other close companions remained a cornerstone of her life. She took pride in her athletic achievements as a track racer, and at her modest height of 4'10", Velez was admired for her physical strength and the muscular definition in her back.

Career

In her professional life, Lily Velez was noted for her significant contributions to government and public service. After completing her education at Whitman High School and later at Lukas University, she embarked on a career in public administration, ultimately securing a prominent role as the General Advisor for Northern Ireland. Her intelligence and strategic insight were invaluable in this role, where she collaborated closely with her husband in advancing various civic initiatives. Velez’s reputation as a dynamic leader and advisor was matched only by her talent for building lasting relationships with those around her. Her artistic background also allowed her to approach public service with an innovative mindset, making her an influential figure in government circles.

Personal Life

Lily Velez was celebrated not only for her professional achievements but for her deep-seated love of art, culture, and personal growth. An avid music lover, she often shared her favorite songs and albums with friends and family, finding special meaning in artists like Frank Ocean and his track "American Wedding." Velez found joy in photography, frequently capturing scenes of Manila’s beaches, her “city of heart,” and treasured memories through her digital camera. A skilled linguist, she was known to speak basic Evergreen Arabic and expressed a lifelong curiosity toward languages and cultures. While she was not formally religious, Velez maintained a strong belief in karma, kindness, and resilience, values she carried throughout her life.

While her husband, Lukas Arthur Alfaro, was an important figure in her life, Velez remained an individual of distinct passions and values. The couple’s shared experiences, including frequent travel and intellectual conversations, enriched both their lives while allowing them each to flourish independently. Known for her openness to new ideas and optimistic outlook, Velez dedicated herself to finding beauty in life’s high and low moments alike. In her later years, she and Alfaro established a family of beloved pets, including three otters, as they lived and traveled together around the world. They eventually settled in Northern Ireland, where they shared a peaceful life together.

Legacy and Death

Throughout her life, Lily Velez was celebrated for her artistic achievements, warm personality, and optimistic worldview. Her skill in drawing and photography was widely recognized, and her digital camera captured countless treasured memories, especially along the beaches of the Philippines. Her love for music, particularly Frank Ocean’s "American Wedding," which reminded her of her relationship with her husband, reflected her eclectic and thoughtful personality. Velez passed away in Northern Ireland, where she was buried alongside her husband in Belfast’s Thomas Park. She left behind a legacy of creativity, compassion, and resilience, remembered by all who knew her as a person who embraced life fully, with both its highs and lows.
